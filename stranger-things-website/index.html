About the Show

Stranger Things is a science fiction horror drama television series created by the Duffer Brothers. Set in the 1980s in the fictional town of Hawkins, Indiana, the series follows a group of friends as they uncover supernatural mysteries and government conspiracies.

When a young boy disappears, his mother, a police chief, and his friends must confront terrifying supernatural forces in order to get him back. Along the way, they discover a mysterious girl with extraordinary powers and a secret government experiment.

The show features themes of friendship, family, and the power of love in the face of overwhelming darkness. With its nostalgic 1980s setting, compelling characters, and gripping storylines, Stranger Things has become a cultural phenomenon.

Key Elements

The Upside Down

A dark parallel dimension existing alongside the human world. A place of decay and destruction, home to terrifying creatures and supernatural phenomena.

Hawkins Lab

A secret government facility conducting experiments on human subjects and interdimensional portals, led by the mysterious Dr. Brenner.

The Demogorgon

A predatory humanoid creature from the Upside Down, named after the monster from Dungeons & Dragons. The first major threat faced by our heroes.
